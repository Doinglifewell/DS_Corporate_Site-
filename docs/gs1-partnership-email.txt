I'm reaching out because DeStore is built directly on GS1 Digital Link as its QR resolution standard, and I believe there may be strong alignment with GS1 Australia's innovation and partner programs.

What DeStore does:

- Issues EU-compliant Digital Product Passports per product sold (not per scan)
- Uses GS1 Digital Link QR codes paired with Laava Smart Fingerprint™ for physical authentication
- Anchors every DPP record to Base blockchain (Coinbase's L2) with an Ethereum mainnet anchor — making product records immutable and tamper-proof
- Issues soulbound NFT ownership records to brand wallets, with transferable ownership claims to consumer wallets
- Resolves every scan to the brand's own domain — not a third-party platform

What we're building next — AR ownership experiences:

We are developing an AR layer directly into our consumer wallet product (DeAR — Decentralised Asset Register). When a verified product owner scans their GS1 Digital Link QR, their ownership is confirmed on-chain, and they are served a native AR experience via Apple AR Quick Look — rendering their product in their physical space using ARKit. This is gated by cryptographic ownership verification, meaning only the current legitimate owner can access the AR content. Counterfeit products and non-owners receive nothing.

This creates a new category: ownership-gated AR experiences anchored to GS1 Digital Link — where the QR code is both the compliance gateway and the AR trigger.
