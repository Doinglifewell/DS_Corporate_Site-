Week 1 · Foundations
What Is a Digital Product Passport?
The EU ESPR explained. What it means for your brand. Timeline. Deadlines. What happens if you're not ready.
90 min
This is the "why should I care" session. We break down the EU Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation in plain language — what's required, when, and for which products. We show real examples of what a DPP looks like, what data it contains, and how consumers and regulators will interact with it. No jargon. No blockchain talk. Just the business reality.

Week 2 · Infrastructure
GS1 Barcodes & Digital Link
How GS1 barcodes become the gateway to your DPP. The QR code revolution replacing traditional barcodes by 2027.
90 min
Every product needs a unique identifier. GS1 Digital Link turns a standard barcode into a smart QR code that resolves to your DPP, product page, marketing content, or any URL — depending on who's scanning. This session covers why GS1 Sunrise 2027 matters, how the DeStore GS1 prefix works, and how brands can get every product barcoded and scannable without a GS1 membership.
GS1 basics for brandsGTINs, Digital Link, QR vs traditional barcodes. What's changing and why.
GS1 Sunrise 2027QR codes replace barcodes at retail. How to be ready. What retailers expect.
One QR, many destinationsSame code resolves differently for consumers, retailers, recyclers, and regulators.
Branded QR designLive demo — design a QR code with your brand colours, logo, and style in minutes.

Week 3 · Trust Layer
Blockchain for Brands (Without the Hype)
How blockchain makes DPP data tamper-proof, verifiable, and permanent — without your customers ever knowing it's there.
90 min
Blockchain is the trust infrastructure under the DPP — but your customers never see it. This session explains how on-chain records make product data verifiable by regulators, how lifecycle events (sale, resale, recycle) create an immutable product history, and why this matters for brand trust. Zero crypto jargon. We focus on what it does for the brand, not how the technology works.
What blockchain does for your DPPTamper-proof records, verifiable provenance, lifecycle history. The trust layer explained simply.
Zero crypto UXHow DeStore handles all the chain interactions. Your customers use email login, not wallets.
Audit trails that regulators trustOn-chain event logs, SHA-256 hashing, timestamped approvals. What "verifiable" actually means.
Lifecycle trackingEvery sale, gift, recycle, and return logged permanently. Product history that outlasts the brand.

Week 4 · Growth
DPP as a Marketing Engine
How white-labelled DPP touchpoints drive SEO, deepen customer relationships, and turn compliance into a revenue channel.

Week 5 · Circular Economy
Trade, Gift, Recycle, Return
How the circular economy creates new revenue from products you've already sold. The business model beyond the first sale.
90 min
The DPP doesn't end at the first sale — it's a permanent digital twin that tracks the product's entire life. This session covers how brands earn from resale (1.3% secondary market fee), how authenticated resale increases brand value, how take-back programs build loyalty, and how recycling data closes the loop for material sourcing. The circular economy isn't just sustainability — it's a business model.
Authenticated resaleSecondhand markets powered by verified DPPs. No fakes, no disputes, higher resale values.
Gift & transferDigital ownership transfer with full product history. Gift cards, loyalty programs, brand engagement.
Recycling & take-backTrack materials back to source. EU ESPR requires end-of-life data. Turn compliance into circular sourcing.
Revenue beyond first sale1.3% on resales, repair services, spare parts. Every lifecycle event is a revenue opportunity.
