Core Technology

Laava Smart Fingerprints.

The secure, unclonable alternative to QR codes — developed in collaboration with CSIRO and AustCyber. Each product receives a unique optical fingerprint that counterfeiters cannot replicate. Unlike QR codes, Smart Fingerprints contain no encoded data or URLs that can be copied. Only the Laava scanner can authenticate them, and every scan is logged with date, time, location, and forensic data.

Brand Protection

Anti-counterfeit detection.

Real-time counterfeit detection and automatic flagging. When a copied or reproduced Smart Fingerprint is scanned, the system immediately flags it as "Suspected Counterfeit" and alerts the brand owner. Reid Fruits deployed Smart Fingerprints on 340,000 cherry boxes across 20 export markets — counterfeit attempts dropped from 10 in season one to zero by season three.

Verified Provenance

On-chain records.

Every product gets a Digital Product Passport — a DPP Soulbound record minted to your brand wallet on Base, Polygon, or Soneium. Metadata stored permanently on Arweave. Consumers receive a transferable Ownership Claim in their smart account. Scan the product, verify the chain of custody. Transfer ownership when the product changes hands — zero crypto UX required.

Circular Trade

Trade, gift, recycle, return.

Every DPP enables circular trade actions. Ownership transfers via USDC atomic swap on Thirdweb Marketplace V3. Consumers can trade, gift, recycle, or return products — all from a single scan. $0.01 USDC per lifecycle event funds the B&B Treasury for future buyback-and-burn. The record follows the product through every owner.

Supply Chain

GS1 global standards.

Industry-standard GTIN-14 identifiers with GS1 Digital Link URIs. Compatible with existing supply chain systems, ERP platforms, customs frameworks, and barcode scanners worldwide. Serialised down to individual units with GS1 Application Identifiers for serial numbers (AI 21), batch/lot (AI 10), and expiry dates. Built for the EU's mandatory Digital Product Passport regulations under ESPR — textiles first wave ~mid-2028.
