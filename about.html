Our Story

Built from the ground up to give every product a permanent identity

DeStore started as an experiment — could a physical product carry its full story with it forever? The experiments changed form. The thesis didn't. Now it's infrastructure.

Mission

We exist to make circular commerce real.

DeStore builds Digital Product Passport infrastructure for Australian brands navigating the EU's Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation. Every physical product sold into the EU will require a machine-readable DPP from July 2027 — and most Australian brands have no idea how to comply, let alone leverage it.

We handle the infrastructure. Brands handle what they're good at — making things worth buying.

Every product has a story. We're building the infrastructure that lets it travel with the product — from the moment it's made, to every owner who comes after.
— Josiah Kotzur, Founder & CEO, DeStore Network
ESPR gave brands a deadline. We were building this infrastructure regardless.
History

How we got here

2022 — Origin
A 150-year-old industry meets permanent records
DeStore was founded by Josiah Kotzur with a deceptively simple idea: give opals — mined from Yowah, Queensland — a permanent digital identity that travels with them. Building it required infrastructure that didn't yet exist. So we built it.
2022 — Discovery Channel
The world's first opal sold with a digital provenance record on international TV
The Discovery Channel captured DeStore's first live sale — an opal anchored to a permanent record and sold on international television. It proved the concept: physical products could carry verified provenance that outlasts any single owner.
2023 — Sony
The first Sony product anchored to a permanent record
Sony's innovation incubator selected DeStore to give a Sony speaker a permanent provenance record — the first time a major consumer electronics brand anchored a physical product to a tamper-evident public ledger.
2024–2025 — Infrastructure
Purpose-built for the regulation — and everything beyond it
As the EU's ESPR regulation moved from proposal to law, we rebuilt DeStore from the ground up — GS1 Digital Link, Ethereum-anchored records, lifecycle tracking, Laava authentication — purpose-built for the 2027 deadline and the circular economy it demands.
2026 — Now
Australia's dedicated DPP issuance platform
DeStore is the only Australian-built DPP issuance, compliance, and training platform purpose-built for brands exporting to the EU — actively onboarding brands in apparel, footwear, skincare, and homewares.
Our Approach

Five things we believe in

◈
Products should outlive their first owner
A product that carries its full history — materials, origin, ownership chain — is worth more at every point in its life. That's the case for circular trade infrastructure.
◎
Infrastructure should be invisible
Consumers should never need to understand records, verification, or provenance systems. They scan. They trust. The infrastructure does the rest.
⊕
Every scan is a brand moment
The DPP QR resolves to your domain. Your story. Your voice — not a third-party platform. Every scan after first sale is a direct connection between your brand and a new owner.
◇
Circular commerce is the default, not the exception
We are not building compliance software. We are building the infrastructure layer that makes circular commerce work — the rails that verified trade runs on.
✦
Built with the right partners
Direct relationships with GS1, Laava, and OriginTrail — the organisations that define the standards — mean DeStore integrates at the source, not around it.
